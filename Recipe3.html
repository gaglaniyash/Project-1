Vadapav

Vada pav is a popular Indian street food that consists of a deep-fried potato patty (vada) in a bread bun (pav). The dish is often served with chutneys and a green chili pepper

Vadapav
Ingredients Quantity
Potatoes (boiled and mashed) 2-3 large
Oil 2 tablespoons
Mustard seeds 1 teaspoon
Cumin seeds 1 teaspoon
Green chilies (finely chopped) 1-2
Ginger-garlic paste 1 tablespoon
Curry leaves 8-10
Turmeric powder 1/4 teaspoon
Red chili powder 1 teaspoon
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander (chopped) 2 tablespoons
Lemon juice 1 tablespoon
Besan (gram flour) 1 cup
Water as needed
Turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon
Baking soda 1/2 teaspoon
Pav (bread rolls) as needed
Chutneys (green and sweet tamarind) as needed
Fried green chilies (optional) as needed
Onion slices (optional) as needed
Sev (crispy chickpea noodles) optional
Preparation time: 15-20 min

Cooking Intructions:

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add 1-2 finely chopped green chilies, 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, and 8-10 curry leaves. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon red chili powder, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  5. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes, 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh coriander, and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice. Mix thoroughly and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool. Once cool, shape it into small round balls (vadis).
  7. In a bowl, combine 1 cup of besan (gram flour), 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon baking soda, and salt to taste.
  8. Add water gradually to make a smooth, thick batter.
  9. In a deep pan or kadhai, heat oil for deep frying.
  10. Dip each potato ball (vadi) into the besan batter, ensuring it’s well coated.
  11. Carefully drop it into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  12. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  13. Slice the pav (bread rolls) horizontally, but not all the way through.
  14. Spread green chutney and sweet tamarind chutney inside the pav.
  15. Place a hot vada inside the pav.
  16. Optional: Add fried green chilies, onion slices, and a sprinkle of sev and chopped coriander if desired.
